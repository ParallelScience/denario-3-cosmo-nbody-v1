The current PM simulation is limited at $k > 0.25 \, h/\text{Mpc}$ by the fixed grid resolution, which acts as a low-pass filter on the gravitational force. I hypothesize that implementing a **Cloud-in-Cell (CIC) force-softening correction (or "kernel deconvolution")** in the Fourier-space Poisson solver will extend the range of validity of the PM simulation. Specifically, by multiplying the potential $\phi(k)$ by the inverse of the CIC window function squared (or a Gaussian smoothing kernel that approximates the force-softening effect of the grid), we can compensate for the artificial suppression of small-scale power. This approach requires no additional particles or mesh refinement, staying within the current constraints, and should allow the simulation to match the Quijote power spectrum at higher $k$ values ($0.25 < k < 0.5 \, h/\text{Mpc}$) by effectively "de-blurring" the force field calculated on the $512^3$ mesh.
