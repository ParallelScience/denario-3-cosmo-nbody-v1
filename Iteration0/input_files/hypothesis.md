The current power deficit at intermediate scales ($k \approx 0.1 \, h/\text{Mpc}$) is driven by a combination of the Zel'dovich Approximation (ZA) and the inherent smoothing of the Cloud-in-Cell (CIC) mass assignment. I hypothesize that implementing 2nd-order Lagrangian Perturbation Theory (2LPT) for initial conditions will significantly reduce the power deficit at $k > 0.1 \, h/\text{Mpc}$ by better capturing the initial gravitational collapse, while simultaneously applying a deconvolution filter (compensation function) to the CIC mass assignment kernel will mitigate the artificial suppression of power caused by the grid-based interpolation. By combining 2LPT with a corrected CIC kernel, the simulation will achieve the target 5% accuracy against Quijote reference data at higher $k$ values than the current ZA-only approach, without requiring increased mesh resolution.
