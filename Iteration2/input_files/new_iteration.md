- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The project is currently at a "Borderline" stage where it demonstrates a functional, high-performance tool but lacks the rigorous validation required for a top-tier computational astrophysics publication. The "5% accuracy" target is met, but the unexplained power suppression at $k > 0.22 \, h/\text{Mpc}$ is a significant technical gap. 

Executing the $1024^3$ resolution study and applying the CIC window deconvolution are not merely "extra work"; they are essential to distinguish between numerical artifacts (aliasing/resolution) and the inherent physical limitations of the PM method. Without these steps, the paper remains a "code report." By performing these, you transform the manuscript into a definitive study on the scaling and accuracy limits of GPU-accelerated PM simulations. The ROI is high because the infrastructure (Warp/Ensemble pipeline) is already built, and the additional compute time for a few $1024^3$ runs is negligible compared to the scientific value of characterizing the resolution-accuracy trade-off. This iteration moves the paper from a "successful implementation" to a "reference benchmark" for the community.
