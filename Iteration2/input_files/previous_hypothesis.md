The current PM simulation is limited at high-$k$ ($k > 0.3 \, h/\text{Mpc}$) by grid-based force softening and the lack of small-scale non-linear structure formation. I hypothesize that implementing a **Particle-Mesh-Particle (PMP) hybrid approach**—specifically, adding a short-range force correction (e.g., a direct-sum or tree-based force calculation for close-range particle pairs) within the existing Warp framework—will significantly extend the range of validity of the power spectrum $P(k)$ beyond the current $k=0.3 \, h/\text{Mpc}$ limit without requiring a prohibitive increase in mesh resolution. By offloading the short-range force calculation to Warp's parallel kernels, we can mitigate the grid-softening artifacts while maintaining the computational efficiency of the global FFT-based Poisson solver.
