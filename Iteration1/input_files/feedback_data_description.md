The observed ~10% underprediction of the power spectrum at $k > 0.1$ h/Mpc is directly attributable to the dataset description's specification of Zel'dovich Approximation (ZA) for initial conditions. As noted in the "Initial Conditions" section of the dataset description, Quijote uses 2LPT, and the description explicitly acknowledges that "Our ZA ICs introduce small differences at high-k." This constraint explains why the simulation fails to meet the 5% agreement target for the full $k < 0.3$ h/Mpc range, as ZA inherently lacks the second-order displacement corrections that provide the additional power present in the Quijote reference data.
